Overview & Thesis

This project focuses on the subgenre of Supernatural Frontier Horror: the Western’s frontier aesthetics fused with Gothic/Supernatural horror.

Supernatural Frontier Horror treats the land itself as a moral catalyst. Ethical ruptures, communal betrayals, and isolation catalyze forces that unmake the frontier’s myth of ordered expansion.

Thematic Lens

Gothic & Western Intersection — the frontier is haunted by violence, unmarked graves, and broken covenants.

Key quote: “the dead return because they were not properly buried.”

Characteristic 1

Moral Transgression as Catalyst — entities emerge as judgement for betrayal, murder, or communal sin.

Characteristic 2

Isolation as Psychological Breakdown — isolation accelerates paranoia and destabilizes perception (see Emma Tammi).

Characteristic 3

Landscape as Supernatural Force — wind, plains, and abandoned structures become active agents of terror.

The Three Films

Eastwood's Stranger arrives and the town of Lago collapses into guilt and retributive justice.

Cinematography: heat shimmer, harsh light; Color: Lago painted blood-red; Sound: sparse gunshots and wind.

Emma Tammi's film roots horror in isolation. Lizzy’s solitude collapses the boundary between psychological anguish and supernatural presence.

Sound: layered wind; Cinematography: wide shots trap Lizzy; interiors claustrophobic; Themes: gender pressure, superstition, domestic confinement.

Coogler's Sinners stages moral temptation. Vampiric Remmick externalizes desire and generational trauma.

Aesthetics: smoky interiors vs. vampiric intrusion; Music: blues as battleground; Editing: tonal shifts destabilize viewers.

Comparative Section

Shared Conventions: supernatural emerges from wrongdoing; isolation magnifies vulnerability; frontier as moral test; communities collapse; revenge replaces Western heroism.

Why It Resonates: institutional distrust, climate anxiety, genre hybridity fuel revival. Tammi links historical isolation to contemporary isolation; Eastwood reflects post-Vietnam institutional collapse.

Audiences: prestige horror fans, revisionist Western audiences, historical trauma enthusiasts; Platforms: streaming, repertory, online cinephile communities.

Conclusion

Supernatural Frontier Horror positions the frontier as an echo chamber where sins reverberate louder than gunfire. The three films analyzed show the supernatural as moral reckoning rather than random threat.

The frontier never stays empty; ghosts of moral failure persist. Hybrid aesthetics unearth buried violence and test human nature where isolation and landscape conspire to judge and torment.
